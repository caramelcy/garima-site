Teaching · 2016 — present

Seven years
explaining chemistry

Research and teaching pull in the same direction: both are the work of making something confusing become obvious. I teach chemistry to school students meeting it for the first time and to NET-JRF aspirants competing for a few hundred places — and the same rule holds either way. If I can't explain it plainly, I don't understand it yet.

Still teaching, still taking students — alongside the research, not instead of it.

Who I teach

Different exams reward different things — IB wants reasoning written out, JEE wants speed under pressure, CSIR-NET wants depth. The chemistry doesn't change; the way you have to hold it does. Teaching across all of them keeps me honest about what an explanation is actually for.
